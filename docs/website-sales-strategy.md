# Mateba.co.za Website Strategy (Sales-First)

## Core recommendation
Build the website around a **conversion funnel**, not around pages.

1. **Hook**: Clarify value in 5 seconds.
2. **Proof**: Show outcomes, testimonials, and trust markers.
3. **Experience**: Let visitors watch a short brand video and hear sample music.
4. **Offer**: Present packages and clear next steps.
5. **Action**: Drive to one strong CTA: book a consultation / request a quote.

## Is “watch video first” the right strategy?
Mostly no. Video should support conversion, not gate it.

- Many visitors will not watch a full video.
- High-intent buyers often want pricing, credibility, and a way to contact quickly.
- Best practice: keep a short autoplay-muted teaser section, but always show CTA and key offer above the fold.

## Recommended page architecture

### 1) Home (primary sales page)
- Hero: what Mateba does, for who, and the result.
- Primary CTA: **Get a quote**.
- Secondary CTA: **Watch 60-second overview**.
- Social proof band: logos, event types served, testimonial snippets.
- “How it works” (3 steps).
- Embedded short video (45–75 seconds).
- Sample music player (3–5 curated tracks by mood/use-case).
- Packages/starting prices (even from-price anchors improve lead quality).
- FAQ addressing objections (cost, setup, duration, area covered).
- Final CTA with urgency (“Limited dates available”).

### 2) Services / Packages
- Clear tiers (e.g., Essential, Signature, Premium).
- What’s included, ideal client/event type, price guidance.
- Add-ons and upsells.

### 3) Portfolio / Samples
- Audio demos with tags.
- Short event reels/photos.
- Outcomes where possible (crowd size, event feedback).

### 4) Trust & Legal
- Terms of Use and policies sourced from your repository source-of-truth.
- Company details, contact channels, and service areas.

### 5) Contact / Booking
- Fast form (name, event date, event type, location, budget range).
- WhatsApp/phone quick actions.
- Confirmation page with next-step expectations.

## Conversion strategy (what actually increases sales)

## Messaging framework
Use this formula in headline blocks:

> “We help [target client] get [desired outcome] without [major pain point].”

Example:

> “We help event hosts create unforgettable dance-floor moments without the stress of coordinating entertainment.”

## Offer strategy
- Present a **core offer** (most popular package).
- Add a low-friction **entry action** (free consultation).
- Add scarcity honestly (limited monthly bookings).

## Trust strategy
- Testimonial video snippets.
- Real event photos.
- Clear founder/company presence.
- Consistent use of official brand assets (logos/colors from repo).

## CTA strategy
One primary action across the site:
- “Check my date” / “Get quote in 24 hours”.

Keep this CTA in sticky header and repeated every major section.

## Video + music integration (optimal sequencing)
Use this sequence:
1. Visitor sees value + CTA immediately.
2. Visitor watches short overview video if they need confidence.
3. Visitor samples 1–2 audio tracks for fit.
4. Visitor clicks quote CTA.

Do not force users to complete video before allowing contact.

## Metrics to track from day one
- Conversion rate (visitor -> lead).
- CTA click-through rate.
- Video play rate + 50% completion.
- Audio sample plays.
- Form completion rate.
- Lead quality (qualified vs unqualified).

## 30-day execution plan

### Week 1
- Define ICP (ideal client profile) and service positioning.
- Finalize page wireframe and copy structure.

### Week 2
- Build responsive landing-first site.
- Integrate brand assets and legal links.

### Week 3
- Add video, sample player, testimonials, and package blocks.
- Connect analytics and conversion tracking.

### Week 4
- A/B test headline and primary CTA text.
- Refine based on user behavior.

## Quick wins you can implement immediately
- Replace vague hero text with a clear value proposition.
- Add visible pricing guidance (or “starting from”).
- Put “Check availability” CTA in top-right and sticky mobile footer.
- Embed a short, high-energy explainer video near top of page.
- Curate 3 best sample tracks (not a long list).

## Bottom line
Your instinct about using video and sample music is good, but they should be **conversion enhancers**, not the funnel gate. The right strategy is a clear sales-first funnel with strong messaging, proof, package clarity, and repeated CTA.
